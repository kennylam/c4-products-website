The purpose of the pattern tracking table is to bring insight and clarity into which patterns are currently in process, ready for consumption, or being added to the C&CS components library.

</PageDescription>

## Understanding the table

<p style="font-size: 1.75rem">Pattern</p>

- The pattern column will link to where the pattern specifications, conversation, and guidelines live.


<p style="font-size: 1.75rem">MCP - Most common pattern</p>

- A `most common pattern` or `MCP` is a pattern that must be consumed by all C&C products. They are notated with a green check mark (✅).


<p style="font-size: 1.75rem">Maintainers</p>

- A maintainer is the main point of contact for any respective pattern. See the table for a full list of designers currently working on the C&CS patterns.


<p style="font-size: 1.75rem">Code</p>

- Once a pattern is approved, it will be evaluated to determine its priority and next steps for contribution. Most patterns will be adopted into the <Link to="https://github.com/carbon-design-system/ibm-cloud-cognitive">ibm-cloud-cognitive</Link> library, but some may be directly contributed to <Link to="https://github.com/carbon-design-system/carbon-components-react">carbon-react</Link>. 
- If there is no available link in the table cell, it has not yet been added to either.


<p style="font-size: 1.75rem">Status</p>

- The status of the pattern will show where it's currently in the pipeline.
    - `Completed`: A status of `completed` confirms that the pattern has been designed, evaluated, and approved. Once published in the PAL site (or Carbon’s community patterns page), implementation can begin.
    - `WIP`: A status of "Work in progress" (`WIP`) means that a patterns' specifications are still being worked on. Reach out to the pattern maintainer for any questions or concerns.


<p style="font-size: 1.75rem">Further guidance</p>

- For more information or updates regarding the table, please reach out to <Link to="https://w3.ibm.com/bluepages/profile.html?uid=2D1739897">Carrie Lloyd</Link>.
